SAFETY GUIDELINE
Document ID: SG-4021
Title: Pump Maintenance Safety
Plant: Plant A
Area: Section 3
Applicable Equipment: Pump P-102 (AST-0042), Pump P-103 (AST-0044)
Issue Date: 2025-03-01
Status: Active

1.0 HAZARDS IDENTIFIED
1.1 Rotating machinery entanglement
1.2 High-pressure fluid injection injury (25 bar max)
1.3 Hot surface burns (motor casing up to 90°C)
1.4 Electrical shock (15 kW motor, 415 V 3-phase)
1.5 Chemical exposure (if handling process fluids)
1.6 Heavy lifting (pump assembly 185 kg)

2.0 REQUIRED PPE
- Safety glasses with side shields
- Steel-toed boots
- Hearing protection (if noise > 85 dB)
- Cut-resistant gloves for seal handling
- Arc flash PPE for electrical work
- Fall protection if working at height on pump platform

3.0 LOCKOUT/TAGOUT PROCEDURE
3.1 Notify all affected personnel.
3.2 Identify all energy sources: electrical (motor disconnect), mechanical (stored pressure).
3.3 Shut down pump following SOP-4205 Section 5.0.
3.4 Lock and tag the motor disconnect switch.
3.5 Verify zero energy: attempt to start pump, bleed pressure at drain valve.
3.6 Confirm pressure gauge reads 0 bar before proceeding.

4.0 SPECIFIC SAFEGUARDS FOR SEAL REPLACEMENT
4.1 Ensure pump is fully depressurized before opening seal housing.
4.2 Seal housing may contain pressurized fluid even when pump is off - use caution.
4.3 Use proper seal installation tool to avoid damaging seal faces.
4.4 Wear cut-resistant gloves when handling used seals (may have sharp edges).
4.5 Contain and dispose of any leaked process fluid per environmental guidelines.

5.0 EMERGENCY RESPONSE
5.1 Major fluid leak: Evacuate area, call emergency response.
5.2 Electrical shock: Cut power, call first aid.
5.3 Fire: Use Class C extinguisher on electrical fires.
5.4 Injury: Report to shift supervisor immediately.

6.0 TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
All personnel working on Pump P-102 must complete:
- Pump Maintenance Safety Training (annual)
- Lockout/Tagout Certification (annual)
- SOP-4205 Familiarization (upon initial assignment)
